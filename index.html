Who are we?

LingHacks, a global nonprofit under Hack+, focuses on combining math and language to help students from diverse backgrounds develop skills in technology, problem-solving, and interdisciplinary thinking. We organize hackathons, workshops, and clubs to introduce students to natural language processing (NLP), spark interest in technical and cross-disciplinary careers, and support them in creating lasting social innovations. Recently, we held the fourth edition of the LingHacks hackathon.

Workshops

We host computational linguistics workshops nationwide, covering everything from the mathematical foundations of machine learning to creating real-world natural language processing applications. In addition to running our own workshops, we collaborate with partner events and schools by presenting workshops and sharing our curriculum.

Clubs

We create curricula for schools worldwide to help them launch Computational Linguistics Clubs. The curriculum includes introductory Python programming and text preprocessing, key mathematical concepts, machine learning and NLP algorithms, tutorials on building NLP applications, and discussions on research papers related to the impact of computational linguistics on society.

Hackathons

LingHacks IV, the fourth edition of the world's first computational linguistics hackathon for high school students, is set to take place virtually in the spring of 2022.
