Who are we?

We are a school chapter of LingHacks, a global nonprofit under Hack+. We focus on combining math and language to help students from diverse backgrounds develop skills in technology, problem-solving, and interdisciplinary thinking. We organize hackathons, workshops, and clubs to introduce students to natural language processing (NLP), spark interest in technical and cross-disciplinary careers, and support them in creating lasting social innovations.

Workshops

Linghacks hosts computational linguistics workshops nationwide, covering everything from the mathematical foundations of machine learning to creating real-world natural language processing applications. In addition to running our own workshops, we collaborate with partner events and schools by presenting workshops and sharing our curriculum. As a chapter under Linghacks we have access to the some of the most experienced individuals in this field and will create workshops to engage both students and faculty to learn more about this rising field and talk and network with professionals.

Clubs

We create curricula for schools worldwide to help them launch Computational Linguistics Clubs. The curriculum includes introductory Python programming and text preprocessing, key mathematical concepts, machine learning and NLP algorithms, tutorials on building NLP applications, and discussions on research papers related to the impact of computational linguistics on society. This material covers more than enough about computational linguistics for any student (or anyone wanting to learn) to be able to confidently create a project using these new concepts.

Hackathons

LingHacks IV, the fourth edition of the world's first computational linguistics hackathon for high school students, took place in Spring of 2022. We plan to create hackathons locally in Southlake or the general DFW area to encourage students to gain more experience in this field in a competitive setting that encourages collaboration betweeen team members to create projects using what they have learned.
